Features

🔔

Ship's Bells

Classic half-hour bell strikes following the traditional 4-hour watch system. Notifications even when the app is closed.

🧭

Vintage Compass

Live heading from your iPhone's compass, displayed in a dark minimalist nautical rose with cardinal directions.

🕐

Brass Chronometer

A beautifully crafted analog clock in the style of 19th-century ship's chronometers, with Roman numerals and slender lozenge hands.

🌊

Tide Prediction

Local harmonic tidal calculation for 14 major ports — no API required, works completely offline.

🌤

Marine Weather

Wind speed & direction (with Beaufort scale), temperature, pressure with trend, and weather condition — updated for your position.

🌅

Sunrise & Sunset

A visual wave arc shows the arc of the sun through the day and night cycle. Accurate to the minute using the NOAA solar algorithm.

🕰

UTC Clock

Always-visible UTC time alongside your local chronometer — essential for celestial navigation and maritime communications.

🆘

SOS Signal

Hold the clock face for 3 seconds to send an SOS light signal in Morse code. Automatic stop after 5 minutes.

🌐

Deutsch / English

Full bilingual support. Switch between German and English in the app settings at any time.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026


216 Bells ("the App") is developed by Arnd Brockmeier as a personal project. Your privacy is important. This policy explains what data the App accesses and how it is handled.


Location Data

The App requests access to your device's location (While Using the App) for the following purposes only:

  • Displaying your current compass heading and coordinates
  • Fetching local weather data from the Open-Meteo API (latitude & longitude only, no personal identifiers)
  • Selecting the nearest tidal reference station
  • Calculating local sunrise and sunset times

Location data is never stored on any server, never shared with third parties, and never used for advertising or tracking.


Weather Data

Weather information is fetched from Open-Meteo, a free and open-source weather API. Only your latitude and longitude are transmitted. No account, API key, or personal information is required or sent.


Notifications

With your permission, the App schedules local notifications for ship's bell strikes. These notifications are generated and stored entirely on your device. No notification data is transmitted to any server.


Data Storage

All settings (quiet hours, language preference, SOS color) are stored locally on your device using iOS standard storage (UserDefaults). No data leaves your device.


Third-Party Services

The App uses the Open-Meteo API (terms). No analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs are included.


Children's Privacy

The App does not knowingly collect information from children under 13.


Changes to This Policy

Any updates to this privacy policy will be posted on this page with a revised date.

Ship's Bells Explained

A nautical day is divided into six 4-hour watches. Each half-hour within a watch is marked by one additional bell strike, up to 8 bells at the end.

🔕

Quiet Mode

Enable Quiet Mode in Settings to silence all bell sounds and notifications instantly. Use Quiet Hours for a scheduled time window.

🆘

SOS Signal

Press and hold the clock face for 3 seconds to activate the SOS Morse light signal. Tap anywhere to stop, or it stops automatically after 5 minutes.
